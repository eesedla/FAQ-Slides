Cleveland police secondary employment, explained

What is secondary employment?

Cleveland police officers can work secondary jobs with permission. The officer is off the city's clock and being paid directly by a private employer. Officers in secondary employment typically work as they would on duty — they carry weapons issued to them, maintain all the powers of an active, on-duty police officer, and can search, seize, arrest, and use force.

What does it look like?

When you see a Cleveland officer in uniform standing outside a stadium, working the door at a bar, directing traffic at a construction site, or providing security at a private event, that is authorized secondary employment.

What is unauthorized secondary employment?

Unauthorized secondary employment means an officer is working a side job without getting the required approval from the department, or working in a way that violates the conditions of an approval they did have.

What approval do officers need?

CPD requires officers to get approval before taking on secondary work. That authorization expires annually on December 31, meaning officers must renew it each year. There are also limits on how many hours officers can work in secondary employment.
